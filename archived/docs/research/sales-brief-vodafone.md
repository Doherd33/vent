## Executive Summary

Vodafone is one of Europe's largest telecommunications companies, serving 340+ million customers across Europe and Africa with FY25 revenue of EUR 37.4 billion. The company is in the middle of a massive transformation: a completed merger with Three UK (creating VodafoneThree, the UK's largest mobile operator with 29 million customers), a 10-year, $1.5 billion Microsoft AI partnership, and a cost reduction programme that has already eliminated 10,000+ roles. Vodafone has invested EUR 140 million in its AI chatbot SuperTOBi and built an agent-assist tool called SuperAgent on Azure, but its voice channel remains underserved by modern AI. Critically, Vodafone's direct competitor O2 Telefonica is already a Parloa customer, and Deutsche Telekom has partnered with Parloa for AI-powered customer interaction, making Vodafone the obvious next telecom target. The VodafoneThree merger integration creates a once-in-a-decade window to consolidate and modernise contact centre infrastructure across two merging organisations.

## 3. Pain Points & Problems

### 3.1 Public Complaint Data

- **Ofcom Q3 2025:** Vodafone received 10 complaints per 100,000 broadband customers -- among the most complained-about providers alongside EE and TalkTalk. Broadband complaints *increased* vs Q2 2025.
- **Trustpilot / PissedConsumer:** 2.0-star rating on PissedConsumer (59 reviews). Only 17% of users would recommend Vodafone.
- **Common complaints:** "12 days in Vodafone's customer service doom loop"; "spent 6 hours on the phone demanding an engineer"; "chatbot is frustrating and doesn't understand"; "should be easier to speak to a person."

### 3.2 IVR & Voice Channel Frustrations

- Customers specifically cite "the single most infuriating IVR" and "extended wait times" after navigating complex menus.
- IVR containment is poor -- customers frequently need to repeat themselves after transfer.
- Voice channel has not received the same AI investment as chat (SuperTOBi).

### 3.3 Merger Integration Complexity

- VodafoneThree must integrate two separate CX tech stacks, two sets of customer data, two sets of processes.
- 29 million customers across two legacy brands -- creating confusion in routing and agent knowledge.
- New agents in Belfast/Sheffield must be trained on both Vodafone and Three products simultaneously.
- IT integration led by Stephen Reidy (CIO) who managed Three Ireland/UK systems -- familiar with integration challenges.

### 3.4 Cost Pressure

- 10,000 roles already cut globally. EUR 0.4B opex savings achieved FY23-FY25.
- Ongoing pressure to reduce cost-to-serve while improving CX.
- Offshoring/reshoring churn creates training burden and inconsistency.

### 3.5 Multilingual Complexity

- Operates in 15+ markets with 15+ languages for customer service.
- SuperTOBi handles chat in multiple languages but voice remains language-fragmented.
- Germany (30M+ customers), Turkey, South Africa, Egypt all have distinct language and regulatory requirements.

## 7. Objections & How to Handle Them

### Objection 1: "We already have SuperTOBi for AI customer service."

**Counter:** "SuperTOBi is excellent for chat -- 60-90% first-time resolution. But what about voice? Your customers still navigate legacy IVR menus and wait on hold. Parloa is voice-first. We don't replace TOBi; we bring the same AI intelligence to the phone channel where your Ofcom complaints originate."

### Objection 2: "We have a 10-year Microsoft partnership. We build on Azure."

**Counter:** "Parloa runs natively on Microsoft Azure. We're a featured Microsoft customer story. Deploying Parloa extends your Azure investment into voice AI without building from scratch. Your team can focus SuperAgent on agent assist while Parloa handles the voice automation layer."

### Objection 3: "We're in the middle of the Three merger. We can't take on new vendors right now."

**Counter:** "The merger is exactly why this is the right time. You're integrating two contact centre operations, two IVR systems, two sets of customer journeys. Instead of migrating both legacy systems, deploy one modern voice AI layer that works across both brands from day one. This simplifies integration, not complicates it."

### Objection 4: "Budget is under pressure -- we've already cut 10,000 roles."

**Counter:** "Voice AI reduces cost-per-call by 40-60% on automatable interactions. For a contact centre your size, even automating 20% of inbound voice calls could save millions annually. O2 Telefonica is already realising these savings. Every quarter you wait, the competitive gap widens."

### Objection 5: "We use Genesys in Germany. Adding another vendor creates complexity."

**Counter:** "Parloa integrates natively with Genesys. We sit on top of your existing CCaaS, not beside it. Your Genesys routing, reporting, and agent desktop stay exactly as they are. Parloa adds an AI voice layer before calls reach agents -- reducing volume, not adding complexity."

### Objection 6: "We need to run an RFP. We can't just choose a vendor."

**Counter:** "Understood. We're happy to participate in a formal process. In the meantime, we can run a no-commitment Voice AI Assessment on a sample of your call data to quantify the opportunity. This gives your procurement team the business case they need to justify the RFP."
