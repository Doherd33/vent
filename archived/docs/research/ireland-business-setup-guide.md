# Setting Up Your Company — The Simple Version

**For:** Darren, solo founder, pre-revenue, building Vent

---

## What Type of Company?

**A Limited Company (LTD).** That's it. Don't overthink this.

Why not sole trader? Because you can't raise investment as a sole trader, and you miss out on thousands in tax refunds. The LTD costs €50 more to set up. Just do it.

## Tax — The Simple Version

### You Have Zero Revenue. What Do You Owe?

**Nothing.** No revenue = no tax. You still have to file "nil returns" (basically telling Revenue "I made nothing") but there's no money owed.

### When Do You Need to Register for VAT?

**Not until you're making over €42,500/year.** Don't register early — it just creates paperwork for no reason.

### What About Corporation Tax?

The rate is 12.5% on profits. But there's a **startup exemption** — new companies can pay ZERO corporation tax for up to 5 years (as long as your tax bill would be under €40K/year). You won't hit that for a long time.

## Free Money You Can Get Back From Revenue

This is the important bit. There are three schemes that can put real cash in your pocket:

### 1. R&D Tax Credit — Get 35% of Your Salary Back as Cash

This is the big one. Here's how it works:

- You pay yourself a salary from the company (say €40K/year)
- You spend most of your time building Vent (that counts as R&D)
- The government gives you back **35% of that as cash**
- On a €40K salary where 80% is R&D: that's **€11,200 back in your pocket**

The catch: you need to keep a simple weekly log of what you worked on and what technical problems you solved. Just a few lines each week. Your git commits help too.

### 2. SURE Scheme — Get Back Tax You Already Paid as an Employee

If you had a job in the last 6 years and paid income tax, you can claim some of that back. Could be thousands depending on what you earned. Use Enterprise Ireland's calculator at sure.enterprise-ireland.com to check.

### 3. Startup Tax Exemption — Zero Corporation Tax for 5 Years

New companies don't pay corporation tax for up to 5 years. You claim this on your annual tax return. No application needed.

**Between these three, you could realistically get €10,000-30,000+ back from Revenue.** That more than pays for the accountant and running costs.

## The One Thing You MUST Do: Pay Yourself Something

Even just €1,000-1,500 a month. Here's why:

- No salary = no R&D tax credit (nothing to claim 35% of)
- No salary = no PRSI contributions = no startup tax exemption
- No salary = no social welfare entitlements building up

It doesn't have to be a lot. But it has to be something.

## What Can You Do Yourself vs What Needs an Accountant?

### Do It Yourself (free)
- Register the company on core.cro.ie ✓ (done)
- Register for tax on ros.ie (Revenue's online system)
- File your annual return with CRO (Form B1, €20)
- Run payroll through ROS (it's straightforward for one person)
- File monthly payroll returns on ROS
- Basic bookkeeping (a spreadsheet is fine)

### Pay an Accountant For
- Year-end financial statements (legally required, specific format)
- Your annual tax return with actual numbers
- R&D tax credit claim (Revenue scrutinises these — get it done properly)
- SURE application

**Budget €900-1,200 for a small local accountant.** Ask for a "startup package" or "first year rate." The R&D refund alone will cover this several times over.

## Your Step-by-Step Plan

### Now (You're Here)
1. ✅ Submitted CRO application — wait 5-10 business days for your company number

### When You Get Your CRO Number
2. Register on ROS (ros.ie) for Corporation Tax and as an employer
3. Open a Revolut Business account (takes hours, fully online)
4. Apply for an AIB or BOI startup account (takes 2-4 weeks, need to go into a branch)

### Within First Month
5. Start paying yourself through ROS payroll (even €1,000/month)
6. Start a simple R&D log — just a few lines each week about what you built and what problems you solved
7. Check your SURE eligibility at sure.enterprise-ireland.com

### 6 Months After Incorporation — SET A REMINDER NOW
8. File your first CRO annual return (Form B1, €20 online)
9. **Don't miss this.** Late = €100 fine + €3 per day. Set a calendar alarm.

### End of Year 1
10. Get a small accountant to do your year-end accounts
11. Claim your R&D tax credit
12. Apply for SURE refund
13. File your tax return (CT1) on ROS

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Missing the first CRO annual return** — It's due 6 months after you incorporate. Late filing = fines. Set a reminder the day you get your CRO number.

**Not paying yourself anything** — You lose access to all the good tax credits. Pay yourself even a small amount.

**Not keeping an R&D log** — Revenue will ask for evidence. A few lines each week is enough. "Built AI-powered deviation tracking module. Technical challenge: embedding search across 50K+ SOP documents with sub-second retrieval." That kind of thing.

**Registering for VAT too early** — Don't. Wait until you're near €42,500 revenue.

**Mixing personal and business money** — Get a business bank account and use it for everything business-related. Keep it separate.
