## 1. Irish Government & Agency Programmes

### 1.1 Enterprise Ireland Pre-Seed Start Fund (PSSF)

- **What:** Convertible Loan Note of EUR 50K or EUR 100K (the latter in two EUR 50K tranches)
- **Conversion:** CLN converts at 20% discount on next priced round
- **Eligibility:** MVP with demonstrable customer validation, innovation, international potential, execution plan, team with domain knowledge
- **Application:** Online portal at enterprise-ireland.com; reviewed by EI team, then pitch in person
- **How Vent fits:** Extremely well -- you have a working product (33 modules), clear international market (biopharma), manufacturing & life sciences are stated priority sectors, AI-powered innovation
- **Solo founder concern:** EI generally prefers teams, but solo technical founders with strong traction can succeed. Emphasise your AI-as-team approach and planned hires
- **Support:** Get BIC support (Furthr in Dublin, WestBIC in Galway, etc.) -- they provide free mentoring on PSSF applications. Next BIC workshop: 19 March 2026
- **Tips:** Frame it as "AI-native MES/QMS for the 200+ biopharma facilities in Ireland" -- lean into the Ireland angle hard

### 1.2 Enterprise Ireland HPSU (High Potential Start-Up) Fund

- **What:** EUR 50K-250K equity investment (10% stake), up to EUR 1M in exceptional cases. Innovative HPSU Fund goes up to EUR 1.2M
- **Eligibility:** Must be an EI client, internationally focused, MVP with commercial traction, innovative/disruptive product
- **Pathway:** Typically PSSF first, then graduate to HPSU
- **How Vent fits:** Strong fit once you have paying customers. The 76-module platform is genuinely innovative. EI loves pharma/manufacturing -- it is Ireland's largest export sector
- **Tips:** Get a Development Adviser assigned through PSSF first. They will guide HPSU application. Do not apply cold

### 1.3 Enterprise Ireland HPSU Feasibility Study Grant

- **What:** Up to EUR 30K (70% of eligible costs)
- **Eligibility:** Existing EI clients preparing to raise investment
- **Use for:** Market research, technical research, prototyping, business plan development
- **How Vent fits:** Could fund customer discovery interviews with biopharma manufacturing sites in Ireland
- **Tips:** Best accessed after becoming an EI client through PSSF

### 1.4 Enterprise Ireland Innovation Voucher

- **What:** EUR 10K standard voucher (fully funded) or co-funded voucher up to EUR 20K
- **Eligibility:** Any SME registered in Ireland (do not need to be EI client)
- **Use for:** Collaboration with higher education institutions -- perfect for AI validation, GMP compliance research, academic partnerships
- **Application:** Continuous call, open year-round
- **How Vent fits:** Partner with a university (e.g., UCD, TCD, UL) on AI-powered GMP compliance validation, or regulatory science research
- **Tips:** Quick win. Apply now. Could partner with SSPC (Science Foundation Ireland Research Centre for Pharmaceuticals) or CONFIRM (Smart Manufacturing Research Centre) at University of Limerick

### 1.5 Enterprise Ireland New Frontiers Programme

- **What:** 3-phase programme, Phase 2 is the core: 6 months full-time, EUR 15K stipend + supports package worth EUR 40K+
- **Equity:** None taken
- **Eligibility:** Innovative business idea with scalability potential
- **2026 Status:** Most Phase 2 intakes closed (Jan-Feb deadlines). Check individual host institutions for late openings
- **How Vent fits:** Good fit but requires 6 months full-time commitment at host institution
- **Tips:** If you missed the 2026 intake, register interest for 2027. Meanwhile, use the programme's blog and resources -- they are excellent

### 1.6 Local Enterprise Office (LEO) Grants

#### Feasibility Study Grant
- **What:** 50% of eligible costs up to EUR 15K (60% in BMW region)
- **Use for:** Market research, prototype development, specialist consultancy, promoter's own research time
- **Eligibility:** Micro-enterprise (under 10 employees, under EUR 2M turnover), manufacturing or internationally traded services
- **How Vent fits:** Good -- you are a one-person company building internationally traded software. Fund customer discovery research
- **Tips:** Contact your local LEO first. They will assess eligibility and guide application. Priority given to manufacturing and internationally traded services -- Vent is both (software for manufacturing)

#### Priming Grant
- **What:** Up to 50% of eligible costs, up to EUR 80-150K (case by case)
- **Eligibility:** Within first 18 months of startup
- **How Vent fits:** If your company is less than 18 months old, this is significant funding. Can cover employment costs, equipment, etc.
- **Tips:** LEO grants are NOT for software development costs directly. Frame around business development, market research, hiring

### 1.7 InterTradeIreland Seedcorn Competition

- **What:** EUR 800K total prize fund. Overall winner: EUR 130K. Category winners: EUR 100K. Regional winners: EUR 50K. Impact Award: EUR 20K. All non-dilutive cash prizes
- **Categories:** B2B, B2C, Deep Tech
- **Application opens:** 5 March 2026 (NOW OPEN)
- **Deadline:** 24 April 2026
- **Eligibility:** Early-stage, must not have raised over EUR 1M in external equity
- **How Vent fits:** Excellent. B2B Deep Tech category. Working product, clear market, strong story (solo founder + AI engineering team). The pitch practice and investor feedback alone are worth it
- **Tips:** APPLY IMMEDIATELY. This is free money with no equity. The competition process itself makes you more investor-ready. Enter the "Deep Tech" category

### 1.8 InterTradeIreland Innovation Boost

- **What:** 18-month package worth up to EUR 67,900, or 12-month package worth EUR 47,400
- **What it covers:** A full-time graduate employee (science/engineering/technology) partnered with a third-level institution
- **How Vent fits:** Could fund a graduate to work on AI/ML aspects of the platform, partnered with a university research group
- **Eligibility:** Cross-border business element helpful but not required for Republic applicants

### 1.9 Disruptive Technologies Innovation Fund (DTIF)

- **What:** EUR 500M fund under Project Ireland 2040. Individual grants vary (typically EUR 1-5M per project)
- **Eligibility:** Collaborative projects -- requires partnership with research institutions and/or other companies. Must be EI, IDA, or Udaras client
- **How Vent fits:** Hard to access as solo founder. Would need to form consortium with a university and ideally a pharma company partner
- **Priority areas:** AI, digital transformation, manufacturing and materials -- all directly relevant
- **Tips:** Long-term play. Build relationships with CONFIRM (UL) or SSPC first

### 1.10 Science Foundation Ireland / Research Ireland

- **Industry RD&I Fellowship:** Places academic researchers in industry for up to 1 year full-time. You could host a researcher to work on AI/pharma manufacturing problems
- **How Vent fits:** You are an eligible industry partner. A researcher could work on AI model validation for GMP compliance
- **Tips:** Approach SFI research centres directly -- CONFIRM (smart manufacturing at UL) and SSPC (pharmaceuticals at UL) are the best matches

### 1.11 Western Development Commission

- **What:** Equity finance and loans for businesses in the Western Region (Donegal, Leitrim, Sligo, Mayo, Roscommon, Galway, Clare)
- **How Vent fits:** Only relevant if you are based in the West
- **Note:** WDC is supporting a "Get PSSF Ready with PorterShed x Bertie" programme starting 23 March 2026 -- a 6-week virtual accelerator to prepare PSSF applications

- **What:** Up to EUR 2.5M grant + optional equity investment up to EUR 10M+
- **2026 Budget:** EUR 414M for Open track
- **Strategic areas:** Digital technologies, clean tech, biotechnology -- all directly relevant
- **2026 Deadlines:** Short proposals evaluated monthly (first Tuesday). Full proposals batched: 11 Feb, 6 May, 9 Sep, 25 Nov
- **Eligibility:** SMEs and startups from EU member states
- **How Vent fits:** Strong thematic fit (AI + biotech/manufacturing). However, EIC is extremely competitive (under 5% success rate) and typically expects TRL 5-8 with some revenue/traction
- **Tips:** Consider the short proposal first (less effort) to test the waters. Hire a grant writer or use a consultancy (Zabala, STRATA, etc.). The application process has been overhauled for 2026 with stricter technical evaluation

## 3. Non-Dilutive Tax Incentives

### 3.1 R&D Tax Credit

- **What:** 35% tax credit on qualifying R&D expenditure (increased from 30% in Budget 2026)
- **First-year refund limit:** EUR 87,500
- **How Vent fits:** All your software development work building the 76-module platform qualifies as R&D. This is retroactive -- you can claim for 2025 work
- **Key point:** Even pre-revenue companies can get the credit refunded in cash over 3 years (or EUR 87,500 in year 1)
- **Tips:** Get an accountant who specialises in R&D tax credits. Document your development work (git commits, CLAUDE.md, specs are all evidence). The credit applies to your own salary/drawings as well as contractor costs

### 3.2 Knowledge Development Box (KDB)

- **What:** Effective 10% corporation tax rate on profits from qualifying IP (including copyrighted software)
- **How Vent fits:** Once Vent generates revenue, profits from the software (where R&D was done in Ireland) qualify for 10% CT instead of 12.5%
- **Status:** Being reviewed in 2026, may be extended/enhanced
- **Tips:** Plan for this from day one. Ensure IP ownership is structured correctly in your Irish company

### 3.3 Startup Corporation Tax Exemption

- **What:** 3-year corporation tax relief for companies that commenced trading between 2009-2026
- **Limit:** CT liability must not exceed EUR 40K per year
- **How Vent fits:** Your first EUR 40K of annual CT liability is exempt for 3 years. Useful once revenue starts flowing

### 3.4 EIIS (Employment Investment Incentive Scheme)

- **What:** Tax relief for individual investors who invest in qualifying SMEs
- **Rate for first-time EIIS companies:** 50% income tax relief for the investor
- **Max investment:** EUR 1M per investor per year
- **How Vent fits:** Makes you very attractive to angel investors. An investor putting in EUR 100K effectively gets EUR 50K back in tax relief
- **WARNING:** Scheme ends December 2026 unless extended. Create urgency with potential angels
- **Tips:** Use this as a selling point when approaching HBAN angels: "Invest in Vent through EIIS and get 50% tax relief"

## 8. Key Positioning Tips Across All Applications

### Lead with the Ireland angle
Ireland has 200+ pharma/biopharma manufacturing facilities (Pfizer, Lilly, MSD, Regeneron, Takeda, AbbVie, BMS). Vent is building for their backyard. No other AI-native MES/QMS is purpose-built for this market

### Frame the AI story correctly
- For government grants: "AI-powered productivity tool that creates high-value jobs in Ireland's pharma manufacturing sector"
- For VCs: "AI-native platform replacing legacy MES/QMS -- the Veeva of manufacturing"
- For accelerators: "Solo founder using AI to build what normally takes a 50-person team"

### Address the solo founder question
Every funder will ask. Your answers:
- "Claude Code is my engineering team -- I act as PM, it writes the code. 33 modules built in [X] weeks proves the model works"
- "First funding goes partly toward a senior hire in commercial/sales"
- "The AI-as-team model means faster iteration and lower burn rate"

### Quantify the market
- Global pharma MES market: USD 3.8B (2025), growing 12%+ CAGR
- Global pharma QMS market: USD 12.5B+
- Ireland pharma manufacturing: EUR 80B+ in exports, largest sector
- Incumbent (Veeva): USD 40B+ market cap, but focused on clinical/commercial, weak in manufacturing

### Regulatory credibility
- FDA 21 CFR Part 11 compliance built in
- EU GMP Annex 11 alignment
- ALCOA+ data integrity principles
- Full audit trail on every module
