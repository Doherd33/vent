Section 2

Pricing Validation

Is €35,000–60,000 per site per year the right number? The short answer: it's conservative. Here's what the incumbents charge.

Positioning clarity: Vent is not a full QMS replacement (Veeva, MasterControl). It is an AI-native floor intelligence and CAPA management layer , built for operators, connecting to QA. This means Vent can be priced as a complement (add-on) rather than a rip-and-replace, lowering procurement friction while the pricing is still well below incumbents.

Why flat-fee per site beats per-user

Enterprise QMS tools charge per user. A site with 50 QA users on TrackWise at $200/user/month = $120K/year. Vent's flat site licence means the cost is the same whether a site deploys to 10 users or 200. This makes Vent dramatically easier to procure and budget for, particularly for smaller sites.

The GMP validation argument

Legacy vendors charge significant additional fees for implementation and GMP validation (IQ/OQ/PQ). Estimated implementation costs: $10K–$50K (MasterControl / Veeva). Vent's cloud-native architecture and built-in audit trail are designed to minimise this overhead, further compressing total cost of ownership.

The ROI story: If a single CAPA investigation takes 40–60 man-hours at an average senior scientist/QA rate of €60–80/hr, that's €2,400–€4,800 per CAPA. A typical biologics site runs 30–100+ CAPAs per year. If Vent reduces CAPA cycle time by 30% (conservative), the cost saving is €21K–€144K/yr per site , well above the Vent licence cost at any tier. This is the conversation to have with a QA Director or Operations Manager.

How 4,000 sites is calculated for the SOM

The ~4,000 figure represents the realistic pool of GMP pharmaceutical and biologics manufacturing sites in EU + US that are:

  • Operating under FDA (21 CFR) and/or EMA (EU GMP) regulatory frameworks , the two most document-intensive and compliance-heavy jurisdictions in the world
  • Of sufficient scale to have dedicated QA teams (i.e., not single-person labs or academic settings)
  • Actively engaged in commercial drug substance or drug product manufacturing, not just R&D
  • Likely to procure SaaS quality management software (versus running purely paper-based or legacy on-premise systems)

With ~2,009 FDA-registered domestic US sites and a roughly equivalent number of EU commercial manufacturing sites, a figure of 3,500–4,500 is a reasonable estimate for the addressable pool. 4,000 is the midpoint.

Honest caveat: A precise, publicly verifiable count of GMP biologics sites globally does not appear in any single source. The FDA inspection database counts ~4,819 total registered drug manufacturing facilities (US + foreign registered to supply US market). This is the best single public figure. EU-only numbers require EMA data not readily available in aggregate. For investor conversations, cite "~4,000 GMP sites in EU + US combined" with the FDA source as the floor.

Why CDMOs are the highest-value early customers

Contract Development and Manufacturing Organisations (CDMOs) operate multiple sites under one commercial relationship. The global CDMO market has ~1,250 total sites with an estimated 300+ biologics CDMOs. A single enterprise CDMO contract covering 5 sites at €60K/site = €300K ARR from one customer. Landing one CDMO = landing 5 sites.

Land and expand mechanics

  • Start with floor operators (€15K, low friction)
  • QA Director sees value → upgrades to QA tier (€35K)
  • Director wants cross-site visibility → Enterprise (€60K+)
  • CDMO parent sees results at one site → rolls out to network

Natural expansion drivers

  • Visual SOP Engine (Phase 2) , new module revenue
  • Training competency tracking , operator profiles
  • API access , integrates into EDMS (Veeva, Documentum)
  • Regulatory submission support packages

The GMP validation premium: Software deployed in a GMP environment must go through IQ/OQ/PQ validation (installation, operational, performance qualification). This is a real cost centre for pharma sites, and it creates a switching cost once Vent is validated and embedded. Every validated system in pharma has stickiness that B2B SaaS in other industries does not. Annual churn for validated GMP software is very low.

Section 5

Pitch Notes: What to Say When Investors Ask

Prepared answers for the questions this market slide is likely to generate.

Q: "Why $200M and not €25M? Isn't 500 sites more realistic for 5 years?"

"The $200M is based on ~4,000 addressable GMP sites across EU and US, not 500. That 4,000 figure comes from FDA registration data: there are approximately 4,819 registered drug manufacturing facilities worldwide with roughly 2,009 in the US alone. Add comparable EU commercial sites and you get a pool of 4,000+ before you touch Asia Pacific. We're targeting EU and US as the beachhead because they're the most document-intensive regulatory environments in the world, and Ireland gives us a warm entry point into both. At an average licence value of €50K per site, 4,000 sites is a $200M ARR opportunity. We don't need to capture all of them. We need 5% to reach €10M ARR, which is the milestone that gets us to Series A."

Q: "Isn't €35-60K cheap? These facilities spend millions on software."

"It is deliberately conservative. Veeva Vault QMS runs €60K–€240K per site per year at typical user counts. MasterControl starts at €25K but scales to €300K+. We're positioned as a complement, not a rip-and-replace, which means a QA Director can trial Vent without displacing an existing validated QMS. That lowers procurement friction dramatically. Once Vent is validated and embedded, we can grow ARR per site through additional modules. The pricing is also designed to give us a clear ROI story: if we reduce one CAPA cycle from 60 to 40 days, and a site runs 50 CAPAs a year at a man-hour cost of €70/hr, the saving exceeds the licence fee. That makes the conversation a budget line item, not a big bet."

Q: "Why hasn't Veeva or MasterControl already built this?"

"They build for QA and compliance teams who control the budget. They have never built for operators, because operators don't have purchasing authority. The anonymity model, the floor-first UX, the 30-second submission experience, none of that exists in their product philosophy because it doesn't serve the buyer persona they sell to. We're not replacing Veeva. We're building the layer underneath it that their customers are missing. Once we establish that floor-to-QA intelligence pipeline, we become the data source that other systems integrate with, not the one that gets replaced."

Q: "How do you land the first customer?"

"Ireland. The Cork/Dublin corridor alone has 10+ major biologics MNC sites: Pfizer, AbbVie, BMS, Regeneron, Eli Lilly, MSD, Alexion, Takeda, WuXi. I work in this sector, I know the people on these floors, and I know the problem is real because I live it. The first pilot doesn't need procurement sign-off from a global VP, it needs a QA Manager or Site Director who wants to see if this works. That conversation I can have next week."
