# Empathy Map: Aoife — Bioprocess Associate (Downstream)

## THINKS
- "If I lose yield here, that's six weeks of upstream work gone"
- "I need to know what happened in the bioreactor before I start this purification"
- "How many cycles has this column done? The spreadsheet says 87 but I'm not sure anyone updated it after last week"
- "This filter integrity test is going to fail, I can feel it"
- "The SOP changed last month — was it the load volume or the wash buffer concentration?"
- "I should raise this as a deviation but the last one I raised took 3 weeks and nothing changed"
- "If I had 10 minutes to prep instead of firefight, I'd catch these things before they become problems"

## FEELS
- **Pressured** — downstream is the bottleneck; everyone is waiting on her to release product
- **Responsible** — she's handling product worth hundreds of thousands of euros per batch
- **Meticulous** — she takes pride in precision and gets genuinely stressed when systems undermine it
- **Distrustful** — of spreadsheets, of handover notes, of any system that relies on someone else updating it
- **Competent but unsupported** — she knows what she's doing but the tools don't match her standards
- **Tired** — 12-hour shifts running complex purification sequences are mentally exhausting

## SEES
- Chromatography skids, TFF rigs, filtration trains, buffer hold tanks
- UV traces and conductivity curves on UNICORN screens
- Paper batch records with 30+ pages per purification step
- A shared drive with 15 versions of "Column Tracking_FINAL_v3_USE THIS ONE.xlsx"
- QC results that arrive after she's already made her process decisions
- New hires shadowing her who she doesn't have time to properly train

## HEARS
- "What's the yield?" — from management, from QA, from upstream who want to know their cells performed
- "Can you expedite this batch?" — schedule pressure is constant
- "The column is fine" — from someone who didn't check the tracking sheet
- "We'll fix the process in the next revision" — she's heard this for two years
- Pump alarms, pressure alarms, UV baseline drift warnings
- Silence from the deviation system — she submitted it, heard nothing

## SAYS
- "Let me check the column log" — opens Excel, scrolls, isn't confident in the number
- "What happened upstream?" — every single batch, to whoever will answer
- "I need the maintenance history for this skid" — calls maintenance, waits for a callback
- "Can someone double-check this?" — because the batch record is ambiguous
- "I'd have caught it if I'd had the data" — after a yield drop she suspected but couldn't prove
- "We need a better system" — to anyone who'll listen, but nothing changes

## DOES
- Manually cross-references 3-4 systems before starting a purification run
- Keeps her own notebook with column cycle counts, yield trends, and equipment quirks
- Calls upstream operators directly to ask about the batch before handover
- Runs integrity tests twice when she's not confident in the result
- Prints SOPs and highlights the sections relevant to her step — because the digital version is too slow to navigate
- Stays 30 minutes after shift to document everything she couldn't capture during the run
- Mentors new operators informally because the training system doesn't prepare them for downstream complexity

## PAINS → GAINS (What Vent Shifts)

| Pain | Gain |
|------|------|
| No visibility into upstream events | Knowledge graph connects upstream batch data to her downstream equipment |
| Column tracking in unreliable spreadsheets | Equipment nodes track column cycles, packing dates, lifetime automatically |
| Filter failure troubleshooting at 2am | "Charlie, show me the last 5 integrity tests for FH-201 and the maintenance log" |
| SOP changes buried in 50-page documents | "What changed in SOP-2034 rev 4?" — instant, grounded answer |
| Cross-referencing 4 systems before every run | One tap on the chromatography skid — everything connected |
| Yield data disconnected from process context | Equipment node links yield to process parameters, deviations, and batch history |
