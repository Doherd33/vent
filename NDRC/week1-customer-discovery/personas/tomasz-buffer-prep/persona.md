# Persona: Tomasz — Bioprocess Technician (Buffer Preparation)

## Demographics
- **Role:** Bioprocess Technician — Buffer Preparation
- **Experience:** 18 months in biopharma (first industry role)
- **Employer:** Biopharma CDMO, Ireland
- **Shift pattern:** 8-hour shifts (Mon–Fri) with occasional weekend support

---

## Day-to-Day

Tomasz prepares all the buffers and solutions used in upstream and downstream processing. His day involves:
- Weighing raw materials (salts, acids, bases, excipients)
- Preparing buffers to precise concentrations and pH targets
- Operating buffer prep vessels (100L–5,000L)
- pH adjustment and conductivity verification
- Sampling for QC release
- Transferring buffers to hold tanks or direct-to-skid lines
- CIP of buffer prep vessels
- Managing raw material inventory and expiry dates

---

| System | His experience |
|--------|----------------|
| Paper batch records | Handwritten entries — weights, pH readings, lot numbers. Terrified of making a transcription error |
| QMS (SOPs) | Buffer recipes are in SOPs but also in batch records — sometimes they don't match after a revision |

---

## Frustrations

### 1. "One decimal point and the batch is gone"
Buffer prep is deceptively critical. A wrong concentration, a missed pH adjustment, or an expired raw material means every buffer he made that day is suspect. Downstream can't use it. Potentially an entire batch is compromised. The margin for error is zero, but his tools are a pen and paper.

### 2. "I don't know what I'm making these buffers for"
He makes 15 different buffers in a week. He knows the recipe, but he has no context about which batch they're for, what stage of purification they support, or why the concentration matters. He's following instructions without understanding the system. When something looks slightly off (pH 7.42 instead of 7.40), he doesn't know if that's acceptable or catastrophic because he has no visibility into where this buffer goes.

### 3. "The SOP says one thing, the batch record says another"
After an SOP revision, there's sometimes a lag before the batch record template is updated. He's caught discrepancies twice. Both times he raised it, both times it took days to resolve, and both times he was told to "follow the batch record." He's lost confidence in the system.

### 4. "Expiry tracking is manual and terrifying"
He checks raw material expiry dates manually against labels. If a material expires mid-batch prep, he has to stop, get a new lot, update the batch record, and potentially re-verify. There's no system alerting him in advance. He's found expired materials on the shelf that no one flagged.

### 5. "I'm the new guy and I'm scared to ask questions"
He's 18 months in, still learning, and the experienced buffer prep operators have moved to downstream (everyone wants to move to downstream). He's often alone in buffer prep with no one to ask. The training system says he's "qualified" because he read the SOPs, but he doesn't feel qualified.

---

## Goals
- Confidence that he's making the right buffer at the right concentration for the right batch
- A system that catches errors before they become deviations
- Understanding of where his work fits in the bigger picture
- Access to help when he's working alone
- Real training, not just reading and signing
