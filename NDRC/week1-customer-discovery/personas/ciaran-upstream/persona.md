# Persona: Ciaran — Bioprocess Associate (Upstream)

## Demographics
- **Role:** Bioprocess Associate — Upstream Manufacturing
- **Experience:** 3 years in biopharma
- **Employer:** Biopharma CDMO, Ireland
- **Shift pattern:** 12-hour continental shifts (days/nights rotating)

---

## Day-to-Day

Ciaran runs cell culture bioreactors (2L seed through to 2,000L production scale). His day involves:
- Inoculating and feeding bioreactors on schedule
- Monitoring pH, DO, temperature, viability, and VCD
- Sampling and sending to QC
- Completing batch record entries (paper-based or hybrid MES)
- Performing CIP/SIP cycles
- Logging deviations when parameters go out of spec

## Frustrations

### 1. "I can't find anything"
The SOP he needs is buried in a QMS with 4,000 documents. He knows the procedure — he's done it 200 times — but when something unusual happens (a pump alarm, a contamination flag, an unusual pH drift), he needs the specific section of the specific SOP for that equipment. Currently that means: open QMS → search → wrong version → search again → open 40-page PDF → Ctrl+F → maybe find it. **This takes 15–20 minutes.** On night shift with no supervisor around, that's 15 minutes of a bioreactor sitting in an abnormal state.

### 2. "The batch record doesn't help me think"
Paper batch records tell him what to do step-by-step but give zero context. If he's at Step 14 and something looks wrong, there's no way to quickly understand how Step 14 connects to what happened at Step 6, or what happened on the last batch, or whether this is a known issue. The batch record is a compliance document, not an intelligence tool.

### 3. "I don't know what happened on the last batch"
Batch-to-batch knowledge lives in people's heads, in shift handover notes scrawled on paper, or buried in deviation reports he'll never search for. When a senior operator leaves, that knowledge walks out the door.

### 4. "Deviations are a black hole"
He raises a deviation and never hears about it again. Did it lead to a CAPA? Did the SOP change? Was the root cause what he suspected? The feedback loop is completely broken. He stops raising minor deviations because "nothing happens anyway."

### 5. "Training is a tick-box exercise"
He reads a 60-page SOP, clicks "complete," and is deemed "trained." No one checks if he understood it. The OJT system is slightly better but still disconnected from the actual work. He can't quickly check if he's qualified to run a piece of equipment he hasn't touched in 6 months.

### 6. "Night shifts are where things go wrong"
No supervisor, no senior operators, skeleton crew. This is when the unusual things happen, and it's exactly when he has the least support. He's on his own with a phone that goes to voicemail and a QMS he can barely navigate.

---

## Goals
- Get the right information fast when something goes wrong
- Understand what's happening with his equipment in context, not in isolation
- Stop wasting time searching for documents
- Feel confident making decisions on night shift
- Actually learn from deviations instead of just filing them

## What Vent Changes

| Frustration | Vent Solution |
|-------------|---------------|
| Can't find the right SOP | Tap the bioreactor on the floor map → see every SOP tied to that equipment → ask a question in plain language |
| Batch record gives no context | Equipment node connects current batch to historical data, deviations, and related procedures |
| Lost institutional knowledge | Knowledge lives in the system, grounded to equipment, not in people's heads |
| Deviation black hole | Full pipeline visibility: observation → deviation → CAPA → SOP change, all traceable from the equipment |
| Training is disconnected | Tap equipment → see training status, OJT records, who's qualified, what's outstanding |
| Night shift isolation | Voice AI (Charlie) — ask a question out loud, get a grounded answer at 3am |
