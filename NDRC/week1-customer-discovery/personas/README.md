## Common Frustrations Across All Personas

1. **Information is buried** — The right SOP, the right batch data, the right maintenance record exists somewhere, but finding it takes 15–20 minutes across 4–5 disconnected systems.
2. **Systems don't talk to each other** — QMS, MES, CMMS, LMS, and process historians are all siloed. No single view of what's happening with a piece of equipment.
3. **Night shifts are unsupported** — The most critical decisions happen when the least support is available.
4. **Knowledge walks out the door** — Institutional knowledge lives in experienced operators' heads, not in systems. When people leave, the knowledge leaves.
5. **Training is compliance, not competence** — Read-and-sign doesn't build understanding. Operators are "qualified" on paper but unsupported in practice.
6. **The feedback loop is broken** — Operators raise observations and deviations but never see what happened. So they stop reporting.

---

## The Vent Difference

Every frustration above traces back to the same root cause: **information is disconnected from the physical context where it's needed.**

Vent solves this by grounding all manufacturing intelligence to the equipment itself. Tap a bioreactor, a chromatography skid, a buffer vessel, a BSC — and instantly access every SOP, batch record, deviation, CAPA, training record, and maintenance log connected to that equipment.

The spatial interface is not a visualisation layer. It's the primary way operators interact with their facility's entire knowledge graph.
