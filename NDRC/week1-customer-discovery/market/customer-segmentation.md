# Vent — Customer Segmentation & Go-to-Market Strategy

---

## The Plan

### Phase 1: Prove It On the Floor
Test Vent informally with your own upstream team at a biopharma CDMO in Ireland. One department, one site, real operators, real feedback. This is not a sales exercise — it's a live proof of concept with the people who will tell you honestly if it's useful or shite.

### What They'll Be Testing
- **3D facility visualisation** — every piece of equipment on the upstream floor, live status
- **Gantt chart** — planned works, batch schedule, what's happening and when
- **Team management** — who's on shift, who's qualified, AI-assisted pairing based on experience and process knowledge
- **Shift lead workflow** — AI generates shift plan, pairs BPAs, routes it to the shift lead for approval, pushes to the team
- **Equipment search** — BPAs search their assigned equipment before going on the floor, review SOPs, process steps, and batch context
- **Personal shift plan** — each BPA knows exactly what they're doing, what equipment they're on, and what to expect before they gown up
- **Change tracking** — any modifications to the plan are logged and visible

### Success Criteria
- Do the BPAs actually use it?
- Does it reduce the number of questions going to the shift lead?
- Does the shift lead trust it enough to use it for planning?
- Does it catch anything that would have been missed?
- Do the operators say "this is better than what we have"?

If the answer is yes — you have a case study, testimonials, and usage data. That's worth more than any market analysis.

## Market Segmentation

### Segment 1: CDMOs (Contract Development & Manufacturing Organisations)
**This is home turf. Start here.**

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Why CDMOs first** | Multi-client, multi-product facilities. Highest complexity = highest pain. Operators switch between products, SOPs change constantly, training burden is enormous. The problems Vent solves are amplified. |
| **Target companies** | WuXi Biologics, Lonza, Samsung Biologics, Fujifilm Diosynth, AGC Biologics, Rentschler Biopharma |
| **Ireland presence** | WuXi (Dundalk, Mullaghmonaghan), Lonza (future), multiple smaller CDMOs |
| **Decision makers** | Site Director, Head of Manufacturing, VP Operations |
| **Champions on the floor** | Shift leads and senior BPAs — the people who feel the pain daily |
| **Sales cycle** | 6-12 months (shorter than big pharma, longer than startups) |
| **Deal size potential** | €50K–€250K per site annually (SaaS) |
| **Your advantage** | You work here. You know the floor, the people, the systems, the problems. That's not replicable. |

### Segment 2: Mid-Size Biopharma (50–500 employees, 1–3 sites)
**Second target. Faster decisions, less red tape.**

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Why mid-size** | Small enough that one champion can push a decision. Large enough to have real manufacturing complexity and budget. Often running legacy systems they hate but can't justify replacing. |
| **Target companies (Ireland)** | Alexion (Athlone), Jazz Pharmaceuticals (Dublin), Horizon Therapeutics, Aerogen (Galway), Amgen (Dublin — though larger) |
| **Decision makers** | Head of Operations, Quality Director, Site Lead |
| **Sales cycle** | 3-6 months |
| **Deal size potential** | €30K–€150K per site |
| **Your advantage** | They're underserved. Too small for enterprise digital twin vendors (Siemens, Aveva), too complex for generic tools. Vent fits perfectly. |

### Segment 3: Large Pharma (Enterprise)
**Long-term play. Not now.**

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Why later** | 12-18 month procurement cycles. Vendor qualification, IT security reviews, global rollout planning. You need a proven product and a sales team before you knock on these doors. |
| **Target companies** | Pfizer, Eli Lilly, Roche, AbbVie, MSD, BMS, Novartis, Sanofi |
| **Ireland presence** | Massive. 40+ sites across Cork, Dublin, Limerick, Sligo, Kildare |
| **Deal size potential** | €500K–€2M+ (multi-site enterprise licensing) |
| **When to approach** | After Phase 1 proof of concept + 2-3 paying CDMO/mid-size customers |

## Go-to-Market: The Path

```
Phase 1: PROVE IT (Now — 3 months)
├── Test with your upstream team at a biopharma CDMO
├── Informal, no procurement, no contracts
├── Gather usage data, feedback, pain points validated
├── Build case study: "Upstream team reduced SOP search time by X%"
└── Outcome: Working proof of concept with real operator data

Phase 2: FIRST CUSTOMERS (3-6 months)
├── Expand to other departments (downstream, buffer prep)
├── Approach 2-3 other CDMO/mid-size sites in Ireland
├── Use case study + demo to open doors
├── Price: €3K-5K/month per department or €50K-100K/year per site
└── Outcome: 2-3 paying pilot customers

Phase 3: SCALE (6-18 months)
├── Enterprise features (multi-site, SSO, audit trails, validation)
├── Approach large pharma sites in Ireland
├── EI/NDRC introductions to site directors
├── Hire first salesperson
└── Outcome: 5-10 sites, recurring revenue, Series A conversation

Phase 4: GLOBAL (18+ months)
├── EU expansion (Germany, Switzerland, Belgium — strong biopharma)
├── US market (Boston/Cambridge corridor, RTP North Carolina, Bay Area)
├── Partner with MES/QMS vendors or replace them entirely
└── Outcome: Category-defining platform
```

### TAM — Total Addressable Market
**Global pharma manufacturing software: ~€12B**

- MES: ~€4B (Syncade, PharmaSuite, Werum PAS-X)
- CMMS: ~€2B (SAP PM, Maximo)

**Biopharma manufacturing (biologics focus), English-speaking markets: ~€2B**
- ~2,000 biologics manufacturing facilities globally
- Average spend on QMS/MES/compliance software: ~€500K-€1M per site per year
- Vent doesn't need to replace all of it — it needs to sit on top as the operator-facing intelligence layer

### SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market (3-year target)
**Ireland + UK biopharma sites: ~€15M**
- ~100 relevant manufacturing sites in Ireland
- ~200 in the UK
- Target: 30 sites at €50K average annual contract = €1.5M ARR in year 3
- Stretch: 50 sites = €2.5M ARR

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What they do | Why Vent wins |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **MasterControl** | QMS — document management, deviations, training | Compliance tool, not operations tool. Operators hate it. No spatial context, no AI, no equipment grounding. |
| **Veeva Vault** | QMS — document lifecycle, quality events | Enterprise-grade, enterprise-priced, enterprise-painful. Same problems as MasterControl for operators. |
| **TrackWise (Honeywell)** | QMS — deviations, CAPAs, change control | Legacy system. Clunky. Operators avoid it when possible. |
| **Syncade / DeltaV (Emerson)** | MES + automation control | Good for process control, terrible for knowledge access. Room-by-room, screen-by-screen. No unified view. |
| **Siemens Opcenter / Aveva** | Digital twin + MES | Top-down enterprise systems designed for management dashboards, not operator empowerment. €1M+ implementations. |
| **Tulip** | No-code manufacturing apps | Closer to Vent's philosophy but no AI, no spatial interface, no knowledge graph. App-by-app, not unified. |

### Vent's Unique Position
Nobody else connects QMS + MES + CMMS + LMS through a spatial interface with AI. Everyone else solves one silo. Vent connects them all through the equipment itself — and puts it in the hands of the operator, not the manager.
